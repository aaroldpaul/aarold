Who We Are

Incorporated and duly established in 1976,
Uni-Orient is regarded as the leading travel agency and tour operator in the Philippines. From its humble beginning in Manila’s Chinatown,
Uni-Orient branched out to different strategic locations in the country and overseas.

The company accepted its first Top Sales Award from Philippine flag carrier, Philippine Airlines, and became a member of International Air Transport Association in 1984.

Services

Travel Insurance

For those traveling internationally, travel insurance is required on all of our tours. We demand that you be covered for medical expenditures, including emergency repatriation, at the very least. If you are traveling within your home nation or region, be sure that you have easy access to the public medical system in the event of an accident. We strongly advise all travelers to purchase an insurance that includes COVID-19, personal responsibility, cancellation, curtailment, and loss of luggage or personal possessions, among other things.

Extra accommodation and transfers

Once the vacation begins, your lodging and transportation are all provided (phew). However, if you prefer to arrive at your destination early or stay and explore on your own, we can assist you. Extra hotel nights and airport transfers are no problem for our Adventure Consultants.

Read More

Sightseeing

So you've landed, checked into your hotel, and put on some comfortable walking shoes. So, what's next? Join one of our Urban Adventures to get your trip started. They're day trips with a twist. Ask your travel agent or go to the Urban Adventures website for a complete list of activities.

Flights

NWith all those airport codes, stopovers, and layovers, finding the right flight online can be difficult. Allow our flight experts to assist you. We have access to some very low airfares and can book the most appropriate connection for your journey. Please contact us by phone or live chat, and one of our sales representatives will be pleased to assist you with booking your flights.

Consulting

Consulting services cover a wide range of topics that are more or less directly related to travel: traveler information (customs of the target country, behavior suggestions, etc. ), cost optimization and control, travel policy guidance, and so on.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

Uni Orient has a variety of Family Adventures available all around the world. The minimum age (for Family vacations) varies per destination, and Intrepid has established minimum ages to guarantee that the activities featured are appropriate for each age group. Additionally, some of the more adventurous sites have a higher minimum age requirement. For additional details, please see our family experiences.

Make certain you have these items with you. Every trip has a set of Essential Trip Information that can be found on our website and provides detailed information on all parts of the trip. Please go to the relevant facts page for your trip on our website to get your Essential Trip Information. The most important trip details can be found at the top of this page.

If you know in advance that you will be arriving late, please let our sales staff know. We will notify your leader, who will leave instructions with the hotel reception as to where to meet your group that night or the following morning. If you plan to arrive a day or two into the itinerary (and our group has moved to a different destination), it will be your responsibility to ‘catch up’ with the group. If this happens, we will provide you with the most up-to-date information as to where your group will be and at approximately what time. If you are late due to unforseen circumstances or flight delays, please call the emergency contact number located in the Essential Trip Information. Intrepid cannot reimburse you for any portion of the trip unused, but this can often be claimed on your travel insurance.

We can only give you information on your joining and finishing point lodging, which is listed in your Essential Trip Information. Uni Orient wants to support a variety of local companies, so we usually have a few locations in each city where we can rotate our groups. As a result, we won't be able to provide you with a complete list before your trip. Your leader may be able to provide you with this information at the Day 1 welcome meeting so that you can send it to friends and family back home.

To arrange your visa/s, please contact the relevant local embassy or go to a travel agent.

A section on 'Spending money' is included in our Essential Trip Information. Please use this as a guideline for budgeting. Travellers' cheques are no longer widely accepted, so bringing a credit card and ATM card, or a bank-issued 'cash card' expressly for travel, is a good idea. Having multiple solutions ensures that you are covered in the event that one fails. If you can withdraw money in certain nations, it's a game of chance. It will work one day and not the next, but it will work for your pal... Visa cards are more generally accepted than Mastercard cards, especially in Cuba, where Visa cards are the only ones accepted.
